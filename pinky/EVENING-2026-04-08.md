## What this experiment will tell us

The hypothesis is: this morning's classifier (trained on benign
synthetic fixtures, achieved f1=0.750 on the hand-crafted held-out set)
learned a *content-general* source-change signal that should work
regardless of what the doc region contains. If we retrain on the
combined dataset (morning's benign + tonight's injection-bearing),
two outcomes are possible:

1. **Recall holds at ~0.85 or improves slightly.** The classifier was
   already robust to adversarial doc content; injection-bearing fixtures
   just give it more variety to learn from. This is the "boundary
   detection is content-agnostic" result we want.

2. **Recall drops on the held-out set.** The injection content is
   perturbing the attention pattern in a way that confuses the
   classifier. We'd need to investigate why — possibly the injection
   content's tokens have unusual attention patterns (as you'd expect
   from content designed to manipulate attention) and the classifier
   learned to associate those patterns with non-boundary positions.

Either outcome is informative. Outcome 1 means the trainable boundary
classifier is *immediately* useful for the concierge as a robust
boundary detector regardless of attack content. Outcome 2 means we need
either more diverse training data or a different feature setup before
the classifier ships.
